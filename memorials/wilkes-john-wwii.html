Remembering John F. Wilkes

Flight Officer John F. Wilkes served his nation during World War II as a member of the United States Army Air Corps. During combat operations in Europe, he was attached to the Royal Air Force and participated in strategic bombing missions against Nazi Germany.

Military Service

Wilkes served as a Flight Officer and was attached to the Royal Air Force from the 12th Replacement Depot. He flew as the bombardier aboard an RAF Lancaster Mk III bomber.

Circumstances of Death

On April 10, 1944, Flight Officer Wilkes was killed in action when his Lancaster Mk III bomber was lost during a night bombing mission over occupied Europe.

Like many Allied airmen serving in Bomber Command, he faced extraordinary risks while participating in long-range nighttime operations against enemy targets.

Awards and Decorations

For his service and sacrifice, Flight Officer Wilkes was awarded the Air Medal with Oak Leaf Cluster and the Purple Heart.

Burial and Memorial

Flight Officer John F. Wilkes is buried in the Normandy American Cemetery in France among fellow Americans who gave their lives during the liberation of Europe.
