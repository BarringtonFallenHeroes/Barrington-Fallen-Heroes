Remembering Frank S. Barnard, Jr.

Frank S. Barnard, Jr. was the son of Frank and Mary McLaughlin Barnard of Barrington, Rhode Island. The family resided at 671 County Road, and Frank also lived at 82 Middle Highway.

```
```

Barrington Connection

Frank attended Barrington schools and was a member of Barrington Congregational Church. He left school during his sophomore year to enter military service during World War II.

Military Service

Pvt. Barnard served in the United States Army and was assigned to the 42nd Field Hospital Corps in Germany during the closing months of World War II.

Circumstances of Death

On September 7, 1945, Frank died from injuries sustained while serving near Koeffering, Germany.

At the time of his death he was assigned to the 42nd Field Hospital Corps and was twenty-two years old.

Burial and Memorial

Frank was initially interred at St. Avold Cemetery in France. Following the war, his remains were returned home and laid to rest in Prince's Hill Burial Ground in Barrington.

Legacy

Although World War II had officially ended, Frank's death serves as a reminder that the dangers of military service continued even after combat operations ceased. His sacrifice remains part of Barrington's proud history of service to the nation.
