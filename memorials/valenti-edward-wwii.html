Remembering Edward Valenti

Edward Valenti served in the United States Army during World War II and made the ultimate sacrifice during the Battle of the Bulge, one of the war's most brutal campaigns.

Barrington Connection

Edward Valenti was married to Mildred Valenti and the family resided at 40 Arlington Avenue in East Warren, Rhode Island.

Military Service

Valenti served as a Private First Class with the 735th Tank Battalion, which was attached to the 26th Infantry Division during combat operations in Europe.

Circumstances of Death

On December 25, 1944, Christmas Day, PFC Valenti was killed in action during the Battle of the Bulge while participating in operations to seize the town of Arsdorf, Luxembourg.

The 735th Tank Battalion frequently faced heavily armored German Panther and Tiger tanks that often outgunned American armored forces.

Edward Valenti was 31 years old when he was killed.

Burial and Memorial

PFC Edward Valenti is buried in the Luxembourg American Cemetery, where thousands of American servicemen who fell in the European Theater are honored.
