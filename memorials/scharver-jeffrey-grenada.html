1st Lt. Jeffrey R. Scharver

U.S. Marine Corps — Operation Urgent Fury, Grenada

Home Grenada

Remembering Jeffrey R. Scharver

Jeffrey R. Scharver was born in Canton, Ohio on May 16, 1958. His family later moved to Barrington, Rhode Island, where they resided at 10 Deerfield Drive. He attended local schools and graduated from Barrington High School in 1976.

Barrington Connection

While at Barrington High School, Scharver lettered in both soccer and ice hockey. His classmates remembered him fondly and voted him “Best Hair.”

Military Service

After high school, Scharver attended Ohio State University on a Naval ROTC scholarship. He was commissioned in the United States Marine Corps and became an AH-1T Cobra attack helicopter pilot. He served with the 261st Marine Medium Helicopter Squadron, 22nd Marine Amphibious Unit.

Circumstances of Death

On October 25, 1983, during Operation Urgent Fury in Grenada, 1st Lt. Scharver was co-piloting a Cobra attack helicopter in support of ground forces. After another helicopter was hit by anti-aircraft fire and went down behind enemy lines, Scharver and his pilot remained on station to help protect the downed crew. Their aircraft was eventually struck by ground fire and crashed into the sea.

Awards and Recognition

1st Lt. Scharver received the Silver Star for heroism.

Burial and Memorial

He is interred at Forest Chapel Cemetery in Barrington, Rhode Island. He was 25 years old.
