Remembering George W. Finnerty

George W. Finnerty was a resident of New Meadow Road in Barrington, Rhode Island. He served his country during and after World War II.

Military Service

Finnerty served in both the United States Coast Guard and the United States Navy during World War II.

Following the war, he reenlisted in the United States Army and continued his military service.

Circumstances of Death

On April 29, 1949, George W. Finnerty died as the result of an accident while performing guard duty at Fort Belvoir, Virginia.

Although his death occurred after World War II, his service spanned both the wartime and postwar periods.

Burial and Memorial

George W. Finnerty is buried at St. John's Cemetery in Warren, Rhode Island.

Legacy

George Finnerty is remembered for his continued commitment to military service, serving in three branches of the Armed Forces and remaining in uniform even after the end of World War II.
