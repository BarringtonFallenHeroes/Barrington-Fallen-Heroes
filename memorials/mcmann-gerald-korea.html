Remembering Gerald L. McMann

Gerald L. McMann was born on October 21, 1927, in Lowell, Massachusetts, to a second-generation Irish American family from New England. His family moved to Barrington in 1943 and resided at 7 Joyce Street.

Barrington Connection

McMann attended Peck High School and graduated with the Class of 1946. Known as “Jerry,” and remembered as “The Golden Boy of Barrington,” he was captain of the basketball team and lettered in football, baseball, and track.

His classmates selected him as “Most Athletic.” He also participated in the Literary, Drama, and Glee Clubs.

Military Service

McMann enlisted in the United States Air Force and served as a B-29A Superfortress crew member with the 344th Bomb Squadron, 98th Bomb Wing, operating from Okinawa, Japan, in support of Korean War operations.

Circumstances of Death

On August 30, 1952, his bomber, “Bust’n The Blue,” took on a second bombing mission. Because of that second mission, the aircraft could not return to Okinawa without refueling.

While attempting a second approach in bad weather, the B-29 crashed into a mountain four miles north of Taegu, South Korea. All twelve crew members perished.

Burial and Memorial

Because his parents had moved to Pittsfield, Maine, his recovered remains were interred in Pittsfield Village Cemetery. He was 24 years old.
