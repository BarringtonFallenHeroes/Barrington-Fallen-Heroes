Remembering Gerald L. McMann

Airman Second Class John Gerald McMann was a United States Air Force crew member who gave his life during Korean War operations when his B-29A Superfortress crashed near Taegu, South Korea, on August 30, 1952.

Known to his Barrington classmates as “Jerry” and remembered as “The Golden Boy of Barrington,” McMann was a gifted athlete, student leader, and young airman whose life was cut short at the age of twenty-four.

Early Life

John Gerald McMann was born on October 21, 1927, in Lowell, Massachusetts, to a second-generation Irish American family from New England.

His family moved to Barrington, Rhode Island, in 1943 and resided at 7 Joyce Street. His Selective Service registration card later confirmed his Barrington residence, listed him as an eighteen-year-old student, and identified John T. McMann of 7 Joyce Street as the person who would always know his address.

Barrington Connection

McMann attended Peck High School and graduated with the Class of 1946.

His 1946 yearbook entry described him as “Jerry,” “The Golden Boy of Barrington,” and noted that he was “well-known throughout the state for his outstanding ability in basketball.”

He served as captain of the basketball team and lettered in football, baseball, and track. His classmates selected him as “Most Athletic.” He also participated in the Literary Staff, Victory Corps, Dramatic Club, Senior Play, and Glee Club.

Around school, McMann was remembered for his good humor, broad involvement, athletic talent, and all-around personality. His achievements at Peck High School made him one of Barrington's most recognizable young men of the postwar era.

Military Service

McMann enlisted in the United States Air Force and served as a crew member aboard a B-29A Superfortress bomber with the 344th Bombardment Squadron, 98th Bomb Wing.

Operating from Okinawa, Japan, the 98th Bomb Wing supported United Nations military operations during the Korean War. Its B-29 crews flew long-range bombing missions over Korea, often under difficult weather and operational conditions.

The B-29A Superfortress was one of the largest and most capable bombers of its era. During the Korean War, these aircraft attacked transportation networks, supply depots, bridges, industrial facilities, and military targets supporting North Korean and Chinese forces.

Final Mission

On August 30, 1952, McMann was serving aboard B-29A Superfortress aircraft number 44-62167, known as Bust'n The Blue.

The aircraft was conducting bombing operations in support of Korean War missions. After taking on a second bombing mission, the aircraft could not return directly to Okinawa without refueling.

While attempting a second approach in bad weather near Taegu, South Korea, the B-29A crashed into a mountain approximately four miles north of the city.

All twelve crew members aboard the aircraft perished in the crash.

Official records classify the loss as a non-hostile aircraft crash during operational service.

Burial and Memorial

McMann's remains were recovered following the crash and returned to the United States.

Because his parents had relocated to Pittsfield, Maine, he was laid to rest in Pittsfield Village Cemetery in Pittsfield, Maine.

He was twenty-four years old.

Legacy

Gerald McMann is remembered among Barrington's Korean War fallen and among the airmen who gave their lives during military operations on the Korean Peninsula.

His life story bridges two worlds: the Barrington high school athlete celebrated by his classmates and the young Air Force crewman who served in one of the most demanding forms of military aviation during the Korean War.

Though buried in Maine, his connection to Barrington remains firmly documented through his family residence at 7 Joyce Street, his years at Peck High School, and the classmates who remembered him as “The Golden Boy of Barrington.”

Today, Airman Second Class John Gerald McMann is honored among Barrington's Fallen Heroes for his service and sacrifice during the Korean War.
