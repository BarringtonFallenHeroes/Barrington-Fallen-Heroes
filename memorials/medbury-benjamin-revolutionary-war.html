Remembering Benjamin Medbury

Benjamin Medbury was born on July 2, 1759, the son of John and Ann Medbury of Barrington. At only nineteen years old, he gave his life during the Battle of Rhode Island while bravely fighting for the liberty of his country.

Early Life

Benjamin Medbury was born on July 2, 1759, into a Barrington family whose son would come of age during the struggle between Great Britain and the American colonies.

As the Revolutionary War reached Rhode Island, young men from Barrington and neighboring East Bay communities were called upon to defend their homes, their colony, and the broader cause of American independence.

Military Service

According to Thomas Williams Bicknell's A History of Barrington, Rhode Island, Medbury was killed on his first day of military service during the Battle of Rhode Island on August 29, 1778.

His brief service stands as a reminder that some Revolutionary War patriots gave all before they had the opportunity to serve for months or years. Medbury's sacrifice came immediately upon entering the struggle for independence.

Battle of Rhode Island

The Battle of Rhode Island was one of the largest military engagements fought in New England during the Revolutionary War. American forces fought British and Hessian troops occupying Aquidneck Island as part of a major effort to challenge British control in Narragansett Bay.

The battle took place after American and French plans to retake Newport were disrupted, leaving American troops under Major General John Sullivan to conduct a withdrawal while facing British and Hessian attacks.

On August 29, 1778, Benjamin Medbury fell in battle while fighting for American liberty.

He was 19 years, 1 month, and 27 days old.

Burial and Memorial

Benjamin Medbury was buried in Allin Yard Cemetery in West Barrington.

His tombstone records his sacrifice with the following inscription:

"In Memory of Benjamin, son of Mr. John Medbury and Ann
his wife, who fell in ye battle on Rhode Island Aug. 29,
1778, Bravely fighting for the liberty of his Country,
aged 19 years, 1 month and 27 days."

The inscription is especially powerful because it preserves how his family and community understood his death: not simply as a casualty of war, but as the sacrifice of a young man bravely fighting for liberty.

Legacy

Benjamin Medbury is remembered among Barrington's Revolutionary War fallen and among the young patriots whose lives were cut short in the fight for American independence.

His death on his first day of service underscores the sudden and personal cost of the Revolution for Barrington families. For the Medbury family and for the town, the struggle for independence was not distant or abstract; it was paid for by the lives of local sons.

Today, Benjamin Medbury rests in Allin Yard Cemetery, where his gravestone continues to honor a nineteen-year-old patriot who fell in the Battle of Rhode Island.

Historical Note

The Battle of Rhode Island remains one of the most significant Revolutionary War actions connected to Rhode Island. It also included the service of the 1st Rhode Island Regiment, whose Black, Native American, and white soldiers helped defend the American line during the battle.
