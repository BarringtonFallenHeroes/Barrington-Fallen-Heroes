Remembering Benjamin Medbury

Benjamin Medbury was born on July 2, 1759, the son of John and Ann Medbury. At only nineteen years old, he gave his life during the struggle for American independence.

Military Service

Medbury was killed on his first day of service during the Battle of Rhode Island on August 29, 1778.

Battle of Rhode Island

The Battle of Rhode Island was one of the largest military engagements fought in New England during the Revolutionary War. American forces, supported by French allies, fought British and Hessian troops occupying Aquidneck Island.

On August 29, 1778, Benjamin Medbury fell in battle while fighting for American liberty.

He was 19 years, 1 month, and 27 days old.

Burial and Memorial

Benjamin Medbury was buried in Allin Yard Cemetery in West Barrington.

His tombstone records his sacrifice with the following inscription:

"In Memory of Benjamin, son of Mr. John Medbury and Ann his wife, who fell in ye battle on Rhode Island Aug. 29, 1778, Bravely fighting for the liberty of his Country, aged 19 years, 1 month and 27 days."

Legacy

Benjamin Medbury is remembered among Barrington's Revolutionary War fallen and among the young patriots whose lives were cut short in the fight for American independence.

His surviving epitaph preserves the words of a family and community who honored him as one who died bravely fighting for the liberty of his country.
