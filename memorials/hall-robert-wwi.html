Remembering Robert Hall

Robert Hall was born on September 9, 1894, in County Tyrone, Northern Ireland. He immigrated to the United States through Ellis Island in 1911.

Barrington Connection

After arriving in America, Hall made his way to Barrington, Rhode Island, where he lived on Clarke Road and worked as a gardener for Mr. W. E. Colley.

Military Service

Although Hall registered for the United States draft in June 1917, he was obliged to serve in the military of his country of origin, the United Kingdom.

In September 1917, he traveled to Toronto, Canada, and enlisted in the Canadian Expeditionary Force's Machine Gun Corps.

Circumstances of Death

Pvt. Robert Hall was killed in action on February 4, 1918, while serving with the Canadian Expeditionary Force.

He never returned to Barrington or to his family in Five Mile Town, Northern Ireland.

Burial and Memorial

Robert Hall's body was returned and interred in Saint John's Norway Cemetery in Toronto, Ontario.

Legacy

Hall's story reflects the complicated loyalties and obligations faced by immigrants during World War I. Though he had made a home in Barrington, he ultimately served under the flag of the British Empire through the Canadian Expeditionary Force.
