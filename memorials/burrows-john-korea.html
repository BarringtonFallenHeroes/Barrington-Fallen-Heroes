Remembering John K. Burrows

John K. Burrows was born on June 29, 1928, and grew up in Barrington, Rhode Island. He attended local schools before entering military service.

Barrington Connection

Burrows is remembered among Barrington’s fallen heroes who gave their lives in service to the United States.

Friends and family remembered him as quiet, dependable, and deeply patriotic.

Military Service

John Burrows first served during World War II before reenlisting for service during the Korean War.

During the Korean conflict he served with the United States Army as part of front-line combat operations against Communist forces.

Capture and POW Experience

During intense fighting in North Korea, Burrows was captured by enemy forces and became a prisoner of war.

Like many American POWs captured during the war, he endured brutal conditions, inadequate food, exposure to severe winter weather, and forced marches while imprisoned.

Circumstances of Death

John K. Burrows died while being held in a North Korean prisoner of war camp.

His death reflects one of the most tragic aspects of the Korean War — the suffering endured by American POWs in enemy captivity.

Burial and Memorial

Barrington honors the memory and sacrifice of John K. Burrows among the community’s Korean War fallen heroes.
