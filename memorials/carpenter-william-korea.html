Remembering William E. Carpenter III

Corporal William E. Carpenter III was a third-generation Barrington resident, Peck High School graduate, and United States Marine who died in Korea on August 4, 1954.

Remembered by classmates as a well-known Barrington athlete with good humor, a pleasing personality, and an ability to make friends, Carpenter's promising future was cut short at the age of twenty-one.

Early Life

William E. Carpenter III was born on March 25, 1933, into a Barrington family with deep local roots. His family lived at 80 Edwin Street in Barrington.

His parents were part of Barrington's lace-working community, reflecting one of the town's important twentieth-century industrial traditions.

Barrington Connection

Carpenter attended Peck High School and graduated with the Class of 1951.

Known to classmates as “Bill,” he was active in school life and athletics. He played football and varsity basketball and participated in the Senior Play, January Thaw.

His classmates voted him “Most Athletic” and “Best Looking.” His yearbook noted that his “contribution to Barrington athletics is well-known” and praised his good humor, pleasing personality, and ability to make friends.

Military Service

Carpenter enlisted in the United States Marine Corps in June 1952, during the Korean War.

He served as a corporal with Headquarters and Service Company, 1st Amphibious Tractor Battalion, Fleet Marine Force, Pacific.

The 1st Amphibious Tractor Battalion supported Marine operations by operating and maintaining amphibious vehicles used to move Marines, equipment, and supplies in coastal and combat environments.

Service in Korea

Carpenter served in Korea with the Marine Corps during the final phase of the Korean War and the post-armistice period that followed the July 1953 cease-fire.

Although the armistice ended large-scale active combat, American forces remained in Korea to enforce the cease-fire, support defensive positions, and maintain stability on the peninsula.

Circumstances of Death

Corporal Carpenter died in Korea on August 4, 1954.

Official casualty records classify his death as non-hostile and list the cause as “other causes.”

He was twenty-one years old.

Burial and Memorial

William E. Carpenter III was returned home and interred in Forest Chapel Cemetery in Barrington.

His burial in Barrington reflects the deep connection between his family, his community, and the town he had called home.

Awards and Decorations

Corporal Carpenter's service was recognized through multiple military awards and unit honors.

  • Combat Action Ribbon
  • Korean Service Medal
  • United Nations Service Medal
  • National Defense Service Medal
  • Korean Presidential Unit Citation
  • Republic of Korea War Service Medal

Legacy

William Carpenter is remembered as one of Barrington's own: a young man raised in the community, active in its schools, known on its athletic fields, and laid to rest in his hometown.

His 1951 Peck High School yearbook expressed confidence that with his good humor, personality, and ability to make friends, he would succeed in whatever path he chose after graduation. Three years later, that future was cut short while he was serving his country in Korea.

Today, Corporal William E. Carpenter III is honored among Barrington's Fallen Heroes for his service and sacrifice during the Korean War era.
