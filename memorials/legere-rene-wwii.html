Remembering Rene Legere

Rene Legere was originally from Pawtucket, Rhode Island, and grew up in the Riverside section of East Providence. His father, Andrew Legere, later resided at 178 Narragansett Avenue in Barrington, Rhode Island.

Education and Civilian Life

Legere was a graduate of East Providence High School. Before entering military service, he was employed by contractor Theodore Ellis.

On January 20, 1942, he enlisted in the United States Army Air Corps.

Family

Just four weeks before deploying overseas, Rene married Vera Wright of Lakeland, Florida.

Military Service

SSgt. Legere served with the 415th Bombardment Squadron, 98th Heavy Bombardment Group.

The 98th Bomb Group deployed to Egypt in June 1942 and became one of the first American heavy bomber units to conduct operations in the North African Theater.

Circumstances of Death

On August 9, 1942, SSgt. Legere was killed in a non-battle aircraft accident while serving in North Africa.

He was 22 years old.

Burial and Memorial

Rene Legere is buried in the North Africa American Cemetery in Tunisia.

Legacy

Rene Legere was among the early American airmen who served in the North African campaign during World War II. His service and sacrifice are remembered by his family, community, and nation.
