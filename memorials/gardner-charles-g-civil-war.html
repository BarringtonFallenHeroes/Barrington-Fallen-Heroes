Remembering Charles G. Gardner

Charles G. Gardner served in Battery G, 1st Rhode Island Light Artillery and gave his life during the final year of the Civil War.

Military Service

Gardner enrolled for service on November 30, 1861, and was mustered into Battery G, 1st Rhode Island Light Artillery on December 2, 1861.

The 1st Rhode Island Light Artillery earned a distinguished reputation throughout the war, serving with the Army of the Potomac in many of the conflict's most important campaigns.

Service Beyond the Gun Line

By July 1863, Gardner was serving as a teamster with the Artillery Brigade of the VI Corps, helping move supplies, equipment, and artillery necessary to sustain combat operations.

In April 1864, he was assigned as a stretcher bearer in the Hospital Department of the Artillery Brigade, VI Corps.

In this role, Gardner assisted in the evacuation and care of wounded soldiers, often working under dangerous battlefield conditions to bring injured men to safety.

The Shenandoah Valley Campaign

During 1864, the VI Corps participated in General Philip Sheridan's Shenandoah Valley Campaign, a series of operations designed to eliminate Confederate forces in the valley and prevent them from threatening Washington, D.C.

Battle of Cedar Creek

On October 19, 1864, Confederate forces launched a surprise attack against Union troops along Cedar Creek, Virginia.

Although initially driven back, Union forces rallied later in the day and achieved one of the most decisive victories of the Shenandoah Valley Campaign.

During the battle, Pvt. Charles G. Gardner was killed in action while serving with the Artillery Brigade of the VI Corps.

Legacy

Charles G. Gardner is remembered among Barrington's Civil War fallen and among the soldiers whose service extended beyond direct combat to include the critical work of caring for the wounded.

His sacrifice came during one of the final major campaigns of the war, only months before Union victory and the preservation of the nation.
