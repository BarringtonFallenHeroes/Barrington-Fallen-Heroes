Remembering Lyman Whitcomb

Lyman Whitcomb served with Company B, 7th Rhode Island Volunteer Infantry and gave his life during the brutal Overland Campaign of 1864.

Military Service

Whitcomb enrolled for service at Barrington, Rhode Island, on August 2, 1862, and was mustered into Company B, 7th Rhode Island Volunteer Infantry on September 4, 1862.

He originally entered service as a private and later earned promotion to the rank of corporal.

The 7th Rhode Island Infantry served in numerous campaigns throughout the war and fought in some of the conflict's most demanding operations.

Overland Campaign

During the spring of 1864, Union forces under Lieutenant General Ulysses S. Grant launched the Overland Campaign against General Robert E. Lee's Army of Northern Virginia.

The campaign included a series of bloody battles fought in rapid succession, including the Wilderness, Spotsylvania Court House, North Anna, and Cold Harbor.

Battle of Spotsylvania Court House

In May 1864, Whitcomb's regiment participated in the Battle of Spotsylvania Court House, one of the fiercest engagements of the Civil War.

The battle lasted nearly two weeks and produced staggering casualties on both sides as Union and Confederate forces struggled for control of critical defensive positions.

Circumstances of Death

On May 18, 1864, Corporal Whitcomb was killed while serving with the 7th Rhode Island Infantry during operations at Spotsylvania Court House.

A contemporary regimental account recorded the events of the evening:

"Tuesday, May 17th. A bright moonlight night. Later in the evening Captain Allen who was on picket, heard a force of infantry moving forward on our right, and feared it was the enemy maneuvering to surprise us. It proved to be, however, a decoy party of our own men. Corp. Lyman Whitcomb was killed."

Though brief, this entry preserves the memory of the final hours of a Barrington soldier who fell while faithfully performing his duty.

Legacy

Lyman Whitcomb is remembered among Barrington's Civil War fallen and among the soldiers of the 7th Rhode Island who endured the relentless fighting of the Overland Campaign.

His sacrifice came during one of the most intense periods of the war, only months before the Confederacy's final collapse.
