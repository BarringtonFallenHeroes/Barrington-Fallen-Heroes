Remembering William H. Merritt

William H. Merritt was the son of Mr. and Mrs. William Henry Merritt of 11 Lincoln Avenue in West Barrington, Rhode Island. He graduated from Barrington Peck High School with the Class of 1931.

Barrington Connection

Merritt was described as quiet and studious. At Barrington Peck High School, he played football and basketball and graduated with the Class of 1931.

He continued his education at Wesleyan University in Middletown, Connecticut, graduating with the Class of 1935.

While attending Wesleyan, Merritt was a member of the football and wrestling teams and belonged to the Chi Psi Fraternity.

Military Service

Capt. Merritt served as Company Commander of 3rd Company, 1st Regiment, 1st Special Service Force, the famed "Devil's Brigade."

The 1st Special Service Force was a joint American and Canadian unit and is remembered as a precursor to modern United States Special Forces.

Operation Dragoon

On August 17, 1944, during Operation Dragoon in Southern France, Capt. Merritt was killed in action while leading his men against German fortifications on the island of Port-Cros, off the coast of Toulon.

His company took positions along the ridges flanking the star-shaped Fort L'Estissac near Port-Cros. After darkness fell, his men moved closer to surround the fort.

At 2030 hours, the assault began. Merritt's men were met by machine-gun fire and hand grenades. They returned fire with high-explosive rockets to knock down the main gate.

With Capt. Merritt leading the way, an assault group rushed into the fort's central courtyard. A fierce battle followed until 3rd Company was able to bring enough men and firepower inside the fort to overcome the German defense.

The storming of the fort resulted in 23 prisoners, but cost the lives of four Forcemen, including Capt. Merritt.

Awards and Legacy

Capt. Merritt was awarded the Silver Star for his heroic actions.

Some of the exploits of the 1st Special Service Force were later depicted in the 1968 film The Devil's Brigade.

Burial and Memorial

Capt. William H. Merritt is buried in the Rhone American Cemetery in France.
