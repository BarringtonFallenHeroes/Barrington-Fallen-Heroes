Pvt. Albert J. Simmons

Remembering Albert J. Simmons

Albert J. Simmons was born on February 19, 1892, in the Nayatt section of Barrington, Rhode Island. He later moved to nearby Warren with his family.

Local Connection

Prior to entering military service, Simmons worked as a textile mill card stripper in Warren, Rhode Island, one of the many industrial workers who answered the nation's call during World War I.

Military Service

Pvt. Simmons served with Company D, 310th Infantry Regiment, 78th Infantry Division.

The 78th Division, known as the "Lightning Division," arrived in France during the final year of the war and participated in some of the most intense fighting of the American Expeditionary Forces.

The Meuse-Argonne Offensive

Simmons fought in the Meuse-Argonne Offensive, the largest military operation in American history up to that time.

Beginning in September 1918, more than one million American soldiers participated in the offensive, which ultimately helped force Germany to seek an armistice.

Circumstances of Death

On October 18, 1918, Pvt. Simmons was killed in action during the Meuse-Argonne Offensive.

His death came just twenty-four days before the Armistice of November 11, 1918 brought an end to the fighting on the Western Front.

Burial and Memorial

Pvt. Simmons is buried in the Meuse-Argonne American Cemetery at Romagne-sous-Montfaucon, France.

The cemetery is the largest American military cemetery in Europe and serves as the final resting place for thousands of American soldiers who gave their lives during World War I.

Legacy

Albert J. Simmons is remembered among Barrington's World War I fallen and among the soldiers of the American Expeditionary Forces who fought in the final offensives that helped bring the Great War to a close.

Though victory was near, Simmons never lived to see the peace for which he and so many others had sacrificed.
