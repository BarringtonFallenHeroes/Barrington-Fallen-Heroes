Remembering Samuel A. Barnes

Samuel A. Barnes served as a private in Company E, 5th Rhode Island Heavy Artillery during the Civil War.

Military Service

Barnes enrolled for service on October 18, 1861, and was mustered into Company E, 5th Rhode Island Heavy Artillery on December 16, 1861.

Battle of New Berne

On March 14, 1862, Pvt. Barnes participated in the Battle of New Berne, North Carolina.

Circumstances of Death

In June 1862, Pvt. Samuel A. Barnes died of typhoid fever while in camp near Fort Macon, North Carolina.

Legacy

Samuel A. Barnes is remembered among Barrington's Civil War fallen and among the many Union soldiers whose lives were claimed by disease while serving their country.

Military service records, regimental histories, pension records, newspaper articles, cemetery records, and additional historical materials may be added to this archive as they become available.
