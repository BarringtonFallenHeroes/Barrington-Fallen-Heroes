Remembering Edgar P. Beaver

Edgar P. Beaver was the son of Edward and Gladys Beaver of 239 New Meadow Road in Barrington, Rhode Island. He graduated from Barrington Peck High School with the Class of 1939.

Barrington Connection

At Barrington Peck High School, Edgar played basketball, football, and baseball. He also participated in the Glee, Operetta, and Business Clubs.

After high school, he studied business at Rhode Island State College for two years before leaving to attend flight school.

Military Service

Flight Officer Edgar P. Beaver served as a co-pilot aboard the B-24 bomber Lady Mary. He was assigned to the 885th Bomb Squadron, 450th Bomb Group (Heavy).

Circumstances of Death

On December 23, 1944, while flying a special mission to resupply Partisan forces, the B-24 Lady Mary crashed off the Yugoslavian coast.

Edgar P. Beaver was killed along with all eleven members of the crew. He died on his twenty-third birthday.

Burial and Memorial

A memorial to Flight Officer Edgar P. Beaver is located at Forest Chapel Cemetery in Barrington, Rhode Island.
