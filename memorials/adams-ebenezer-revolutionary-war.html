Remembering Ebenezer Adams

Ebenezer Adams was among the young men of Barrington who served in the Continental Army during the American Revolution. Though only nineteen years old, he answered the call to secure the independence of the United States.

Early Life

Ebenezer Adams was born in Barrington, Rhode Island, on October 13, 1762, the son of James and Lydia Adams.

The Adams family was among the early families of Barrington whose members contributed to the civic and military life of the community throughout the colonial and Revolutionary periods.

Ebenezer grew up during a period of increasing tension between the American colonies and Great Britain, eventually coming of age during the Revolutionary War.

Military Service

Adams entered the Continental service during the struggle for American independence.

Like thousands of young patriots from New England, he left home to serve in the army fighting to establish a new nation founded upon the principles of liberty and self-government.

West Point

During the latter years of the war, Adams served at West Point, New York, one of the most strategically important military positions held by the Continental Army.

Commanding the Hudson River, West Point was considered vital to the defense of the American cause and remained an important military post throughout the Revolution.

Circumstances of Death

On August 1, 1782, Ebenezer Adams died while in the Continental service at West Point.

Although the decisive American victory at Yorktown had occurred the previous year, Continental soldiers remained on active duty across the new nation. Adams died while serving at West Point only months before the signing of the Treaty of Paris formally ended the Revolutionary War.

He was nineteen years old.

Burial and Memorial

Adams was returned to Barrington and buried in Prince's Hill Cemetery.

His gravestone bears a moving epitaph that has endured for more than two centuries:

"Ye blooming youths who on this stone.
Learn early death may be your own."

Legacy

Ebenezer Adams is remembered among Barrington's Revolutionary War fallen and among the young patriots who sacrificed their futures in the cause of American independence.

Though he did not live to see the peace that followed, his service helped secure the freedoms enjoyed by future generations of Americans.

Today, Ebenezer Adams rests in Prince's Hill Cemetery, where his gravestone continues to remind visitors of the sacrifices made by Barrington's young patriots during the struggle for American independence.
