Remembering Peter Harrower

Peter Harrower was the son of the late Peter Harrower, Sr. and Hazel (Bovee) Harrower. He resided at 97 Alfred Drowne Road in Barrington, Rhode Island.

Barrington Connection

Peter graduated from Barrington Peck High School with the Class of 1935. During his school years he participated in the Glee Club and played baseball, basketball, and football.

He served as captain of the football team during his senior year.

Prior to entering the Navy, Peter was employed as a draftsman at the Rhode Island Lace Works in Barrington.

Military Service

Gunner's Mate 1st Class Harrower served aboard the destroyer USS Morrison (DD-560) during World War II.

Circumstances of Death

On May 4, 1945, USS Morrison was sunk off the coast of Okinawa during attacks by Japanese kamikaze aircraft.

Gunner's Mate 1st Class Harrower was killed in action during the sinking.

He was 26 years old.

Burial and Memorial

Peter Harrower is memorialized at Prince's Hill Cemetery in Barrington, Rhode Island.

Legacy

Peter Harrower is remembered among Barrington's World War II fallen and among the sailors who served in the Pacific Theater during the final months of the war.
