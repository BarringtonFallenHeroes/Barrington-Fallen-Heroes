Remembering Peter E. D'Elia

Peter E. D'Elia was the son of Italian immigrants Giuseppe and Carmella D'Elia and resided at 69 Anoaka Avenue in Barrington, Rhode Island.

Family and Community

Peter was the husband of Filomena (Pompeii) D'Elia. The D'Elia family were congregants of Holy Angels Church in Barrington.

Before entering military service, Peter worked for Worthington Meat Packers in Providence.

Military Service

Pvt. D'Elia served with the 351st Infantry Regiment, 88th Infantry Division, during the Italian Campaign of World War II.

The 88th Infantry Division was heavily engaged in combat operations in Italy and sustained more than 6,000 casualties during a forty-five day period of fighting by November 1944.

Circumstances of Death

On October 12, 1944, Pvt. D'Elia was killed in action during the Battle of Gesso, Italy.

Burial and Memorial

Pvt. D'Elia is buried in the Florence American Cemetery in Italy.

He is also memorialized at Santa Maria del Campo Cemetery in Barrington, Rhode Island.

Legacy

Peter D'Elia is remembered among Barrington's World War II fallen and among the many Italian-American families whose sons answered the call to serve during the nation's time of need.
