Sgt. Charles Rapoza

United States Army — World War II

Remembering Charles Rapoza

Charles Rapoza resided at 159 Foote Street in Barrington, Rhode Island. He was the son of Mr. and Mrs. Louis Rapoza of 123 Bay View Avenue in Bristol, Rhode Island.

Family

Charles was the husband of Margie (Conte) Rapoza.

At the time of his death, he left behind his wife and their 28-month-old son, Charles Rapoza, Jr.

Civilian Life

Prior to entering military service, Rapoza was employed by the Blount Ice Company in Barrington.

Military Service

Sgt. Rapoza served with the 30th Infantry Regiment, 3rd Infantry Division, during World War II.

The 3rd Infantry Division was one of the most highly decorated American divisions of the war and participated in campaigns across North Africa, Sicily, Italy, France, and Germany.

Battle of the Colmar Pocket

During January 1945, Sgt. Rapoza participated in combat operations to eliminate German forces in the Colmar Pocket of Alsace, France.

The battle was fought during severe winter conditions and represented one of the final major Allied offensives on the Western Front before the invasion of Germany.

Circumstances of Death

On January 28, 1945, Sgt. Charles Rapoza was killed in action while serving with the 30th Infantry Regiment during operations to clear the Colmar Pocket.

He was 25 years old.

Burial and Memorial

Sgt. Rapoza is buried in the Epinal American Cemetery in France.

Legacy

Charles Rapoza is remembered among Barrington's World War II fallen and among the soldiers of the 3rd Infantry Division who helped liberate France and defeat Nazi Germany.
