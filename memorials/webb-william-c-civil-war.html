Remembering William C. Webb

William C. Webb served with Company D, 2nd Rhode Island Volunteer Infantry, one of Rhode Island's earliest Civil War regiments.

Military Service

Webb enrolled and was mustered into service on June 5, 1861, as a private in Company D, 2nd Rhode Island Volunteer Infantry.

On November 1, 1862, he was promoted from private to sergeant.

In August 1863, he was assigned duty as regimental provost sergeant, a position he held until November 1863.

Veteran Volunteer

On December 26, 1863, Sgt. Webb re-mustered as a veteran volunteer, continuing his service after already enduring years of war.

In February 1864, he was absent for thirty-one days as a veteran volunteer before returning to duty.

Battle of the Wilderness

In May 1864, the 2nd Rhode Island Infantry entered the Overland Campaign, where Union forces under Lt. Gen. Ulysses S. Grant began a relentless offensive against the Army of Northern Virginia.

The Battle of the Wilderness was fought in dense Virginia woods, where smoke, fire, confusion, and close-range combat made the fighting especially brutal.

Circumstances of Death

On May 7, 1864, Sgt. William C. Webb was killed in action during the Battle of the Wilderness.

Legacy

William C. Webb is remembered among Barrington's Civil War fallen and among the veteran soldiers who continued to serve even after years of campaigning.

His service from 1861 through 1864 reflects sustained dedication to the Union cause through some of the most difficult years of the Civil War.
