Remembering Albert W. Adams

Albert W. Adams was the son of Earl R. Adams and Kathleen Healy Adams of 132 Bay Spring Avenue in Barrington, Rhode Island. He attended Peck High School and was a member of St. Luke's Church before entering military service during World War II.

```
```

Barrington Connection

Albert grew up in the Bay Spring section of Barrington and answered his nation's call during World War II by enlisting in the United States Navy.

The Adams family paid a profound price during World War II. Albert's younger brother, Earl R. Adams, Jr., lost his life aboard the Liberty Ship SS Henry Bacon at only sixteen years of age. The family ultimately sacrificed two sons in service to their country.

Military Service

Albert served as a Baker 3rd Class aboard the submarine USS Trout (SS-202), one of the most successful submarines of the Pacific War.

USS Trout completed numerous combat patrols and participated in operations throughout the Pacific Theater.

Circumstances of Death

During its eleventh war patrol, USS Trout operated near Okinawa as American forces closed in on Japan.

On or about April 8, 1944, the submarine was attacked and sunk by Japanese naval forces. All eighty-one officers and crewmen aboard were lost.

Albert W. Adams was twenty-four years old.

Family Sacrifice

Few Barrington families endured a greater wartime loss than the Adams family. During World War II they lost both sons, Earl R. Adams, Jr. and Albert W. Adams, in separate incidents while serving their nation.

>

Albert was lost aboard USS Trout in the Pacific, while Earl perished aboard SS Henry Bacon off the coast of Norway. Their story remains one of the most poignant examples of sacrifice preserved in Barrington's military history.

Burial and Memorial

Because USS Trout was lost at sea with all hands, Albert W. Adams has no known grave. He is honored among the missing and remembered by his family, community, and nation.
