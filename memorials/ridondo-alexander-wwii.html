Remembering Alexander Ridondo

Private First Class Alexander Ridondo served with the United States Army during World War II and gave his life during combat operations in France in the fall of 1944.

Military Service

PFC Ridondo served with the 104th Infantry Regiment, 26th Infantry Division, as Allied forces fought their way across France and toward Germany.

Circumstances of Death

On October 22, 1944, PFC Ridondo was killed in action during the clearing of the Moncourt Woods in France.

American forces faced fierce resistance from fourteen German pillboxes and elements of the heavily entrenched German 11th Panzer Division.

The battle was part of the difficult fighting that characterized the Lorraine Campaign in northeastern France.

Burial and Memorial

PFC Alexander Ridondo is buried at the Lorraine American Cemetery in France, where thousands of American servicemen who fell in the European Theater are honored.
