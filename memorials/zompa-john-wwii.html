Remembering John T. Zompa

John T. Zompa was a Barrington resident whose family lived at 23 County Road. He was the brother of Michael Zompa, who also gave his life during World War II.

Military Service

John T. Zompa served in the United States Navy as a Machinist's Mate 2nd Class aboard USS McKean, a high-speed transport designated APD-5.

Circumstances of Death

On November 17, 1943, USS McKean was transporting Marines to Bougainville in the Solomon Islands when the ship was struck by a torpedo and subsequently sank.

John T. Zompa was killed in the sinking. His body was lost at sea.

Family Sacrifice

The Zompa family endured the loss of two sons during World War II: John T. Zompa in 1943 and his brother, Tech Sgt. Michael Zompa, in 1944.
