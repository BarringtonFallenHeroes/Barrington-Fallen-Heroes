Remembering Franklin N. Crowell, Jr.

Franklin N. Crowell, Jr. was the son of Franklin and Marion Crowell and lived at 19 Highland Avenue in Barrington, Rhode Island.

Military Service

Crowell entered the Army as a flying cadet in January 1943 and later became a bomber pilot with the Fifteenth Air Force operating from Italy.

During the final months of the Italian Campaign, he flew bombing missions intended to soften German defenses protecting the approaches to Bologna.

Combat Experience

In letters written to his parents, Crowell recounted having to bring severely damaged aircraft safely back from three previous combat missions.

Circumstances of Death

On April 18, 1945, 2LT Franklin N. Crowell, Jr. was killed in action while flying his twentieth bombing mission.

He was 21 years old.

Memorial

A memorial to Franklin N. Crowell, Jr. is located at Brookdale Cemetery in Dedham, Massachusetts.

Memorial banners, photographs, letters, military records, newspaper articles, and additional historical materials may be added to this archive as they become available.
