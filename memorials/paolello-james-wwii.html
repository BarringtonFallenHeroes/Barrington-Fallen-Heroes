Pvt. James Paolello

United States Army — World War II

Home World War II

Remembering James Paolello

James Paolello resided at 26 Kirby Street in Barrington, Rhode Island. He was the son of Mrs. Josephine Paolello.

Family

James was the brother-in-law of Alexander Ridondo, another Barrington serviceman who was killed in action in October 1944 while serving in the European Theater.

The losses suffered by the Ridondo and Paolello families illustrate the profound impact World War II had on Barrington's close-knit community.

Battle of the Bulge

During the winter of 1944–1945, his unit participated in operations associated with the Battle of the Bulge, Germany's final major offensive on the Western Front.

Circumstances of Death

On January 15, 1945, Pvt. James Paolello was killed in action during the Battle of the Bulge.

He was 22 years old.

Burial and Memorial

Pvt. Paolello is buried in the Henri-Chapelle American Cemetery in Belgium.

Legacy

James Paolello is remembered among Barrington's World War II fallen and among the soldiers who endured the harsh winter fighting that characterized the Battle of the Bulge.
