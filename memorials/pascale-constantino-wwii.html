Remembering Constantine Pascale, Jr.

Constantine Pascale, Jr. was born on August 7, 1918. Census records place him at 75 Sowams Road in Barrington, Rhode Island. He attended Leander R. Peck High School and graduated with the Class of 1939.

Barrington Connection

Known to classmates as "Pascie," Pascale served as Class Vice-President and was remembered as an active and accomplished member of his school community.

His yearbook entry noted that he had left Peck for two years before returning to graduate with his class.

School Activities

At Leander R. Peck High School, Pascale participated in orchestra, football, baseball, the Arrow Literary Staff, the Arrow Business Staff, Glee Club, and Music Festival.

He was also a member of the Rhode Island Honor Society.

Interests and Aspirations

Pascale was described in his yearbook as an accomplished violin player. His yearbook also noted that he had attended the School of Design and hoped to continue his studies there after graduation.

His senior quote was: "Ambition has no rest."

World War II Memorial Record

Constantine Pascale, Jr. is listed on the Leander R. Peck High School World War II memorial plaque erected by the Class of 1945 to honor former students who gave their lives during the Second World War.

His name also appears on Barrington's municipal World War II memorial, further indicating that he was recognized by the community as a World War II service death.

Continuing Research

Despite his inclusion on multiple Barrington memorials, the details of Pascale's military service and circumstances of death have not yet been fully documented.

Researchers continue to search military records, casualty lists, cemetery records, newspaper archives, and family histories in hopes of completing the record of his service and sacrifice.
