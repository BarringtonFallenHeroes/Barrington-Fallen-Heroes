Remembering Specialist Fourth Class Arthur McQuade

Arthur McQuade Jr. grew up in Barrington, Rhode Island, residing with his family at 51 Washington Road.

Barrington Connection

Specialist Fourth Class Arthur McQuade was born on November 30, 1949, to a third-generation English and Irish American family from East Providence, Rhode Island. His parents owned and operated McQuade's Market in Riverside before the family later moved to 51 Washington Road in Barrington.

Arthur attended Barrington schools and became well known throughout the community. Friends later remembered stopping by his father's neighborhood grocery store, attending dances at Holy Angels Church, and enjoying his quick wit and easygoing personality in the classroom. Like many young men of his generation, he answered his nation's call during one of America's most difficult wars.

Military Service

On his eighteenth birthday, November 30, 1967, Arthur enlisted in the United States Army. Following infantry training, he deployed to the Republic of Vietnam in February 1968.

He served as an infantryman with the 205th Aviation Company, 11th Aviation Battalion, 12th Aviation Group, 1st Aviation Brigade. Fellow soldiers remembered him as one of the youngest members of the unit but also one of its bravest.

Final Mission

On April 29, 1968, Specialist Fourth Class McQuade was supporting a reconnaissance mission of the 1st Infantry Division in Binh Duong Province, South Vietnam, when his unit came under intense hostile small-arms fire.

Without hesitation, Arthur laid down protective fire to shield the reconnaissance team and enable fellow soldiers to maneuver. During the engagement he was mortally wounded by enemy fire. His courage and devotion to his comrades reflected the highest traditions of military service.

Arthur was only eighteen years old when he made the ultimate sacrifice for his country.

Burial and Memorial

Specialist Fourth Class Arthur McQuade is buried at Gate of Heaven Cemetery in East Providence, Rhode Island. His name is forever memorialized among Barrington's Vietnam War fallen and on the Vietnam Veterans Memorial in Washington, D.C.

Legacy

Arthur McQuade's story reminds us that many Americans who served in Vietnam were barely adults when they entered combat. His willingness to risk—and ultimately give—his life protecting fellow soldiers at just eighteen years of age stands as a lasting testament to courage, selflessness, and devotion to duty.
