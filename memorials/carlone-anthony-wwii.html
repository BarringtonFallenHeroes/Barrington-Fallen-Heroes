Pvt. Anthony Carlone

Remembering Anthony Carlone

Anthony Carlone was the son of Italian immigrants, Domenic and Mary Carlone, of 91 South Street in Barrington, Rhode Island.

Barrington Connection

Anthony attended Barrington Peck High School and later worked at the O'Bannon Mill operated by Collins & Aikman in West Barrington.

Military Service

Pvt. Carlone served in the 142nd Infantry Regiment, 36th Infantry Division, during World War II.

Circumstances of Death

On October 18, 1944, Anthony Carlone was killed in action during the Battle for Bruyères, France.

He was 24 years old.

Burial and Memorial

Pvt. Carlone is buried in the Epinal American Cemetery in France, where many American soldiers who gave their lives during the liberation of Europe are honored.

Legacy

Anthony Carlone is remembered among Barrington's World War II fallen and among the young men who left their homes, families, and careers to serve their nation during its time of greatest need.
