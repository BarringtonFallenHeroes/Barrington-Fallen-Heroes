Remembering Amos Cabral

Amos Cabral was the son of Portuguese immigrants and spent his youth on Beach Terrace in Warren, Rhode Island. Later, he resided at 45 County Road in Barrington, in the area known locally as "between the bridges."

Family and Community

Amos was employed as a plumber and pipe fitter before entering military service. His parents operated the Silver Creek Dairy.

He left behind his wife, Lucy (Dorzi) Cabral, a daughter, Sally, and a son, Amos, Jr.

Military Service

Pvt. Cabral served in the 44th Infantry Regiment of the 6th Armored Division during World War II.

Circumstances of Death

Amos Cabral was killed in action on February 7, 1945, while serving with the 44th Infantry Regiment.

He was 27 years old.

Burial and Memorial

Pvt. Cabral is buried in the Luxembourg American Cemetery, where many American servicemen who gave their lives during the European campaign are honored.

Legacy

Amos Cabral's sacrifice is remembered by his family, descendants, and the communities of Barrington and Warren, Rhode Island.
