Remembering Frank J. Chianese

Frank J. Chianese was the son of Italian immigrants John and Vittoria Chianese. The family lived at 178 Whipple Street in Barrington, Rhode Island.

Barrington Connection

Frank was a graduate of Barrington Peck High School. He was the first Barrington resident killed during World War II.

Chianese Field in Barrington, Rhode Island is named in his honor.

Military Service

Chianese served as a Seaman 1st Class in the United States Naval Reserve during World War II.

Circumstances of Death

On July 10, 1943, Seaman 1st Class Frank J. Chianese was killed in action during amphibious operations in the Allied invasion of Sicily, also known as Operation Husky.

Burial and Memorial

Frank J. Chianese is buried in the Sicily-Rome American Cemetery. He is also remembered locally through Chianese Field in Barrington.
