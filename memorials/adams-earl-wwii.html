Remembering Earl R. Adams, Jr.

Earl R. Adams, Jr. was the son of Earl R. Adams and Kathleen Healy Adams of 132 Bay Spring Avenue in Barrington, Rhode Island. He attended Barrington schools and was raised in a family deeply connected to the community.

Barrington Connection

Earl's family suffered a tremendous sacrifice during World War II. His older brother, Albert W. Adams, was lost aboard the submarine USS Trout in April 1945. The Adams family therefore lost two sons in service to their nation.

Military Service

Earl R. Adams, Jr. served in the United States Merchant Marine, which played a vital role transporting troops, supplies, fuel, and equipment across dangerous wartime seas.

Circumstances of Death

On February 22, 1945, Earl was serving aboard the Liberty Ship SS Henry Bacon when it came under attack in the Barents Sea off the coast of Norway.

During the attack, the ship was struck by German torpedoes. While assisting in the evacuation of passengers and crew, Earl lost his life. His actions helped save others during the sinking.

He was 21 years old.

Burial and Memorial

Earl R. Adams, Jr. is remembered among Barrington's World War II fallen and among the Merchant Mariners whose service and sacrifice were essential to Allied victory.
