Remembering Earl R. Adams, Jr.

Earl R. Adams, Jr. was the son of Earl R. Adams and Kathleen Healy Adams of 132 Bay Spring Avenue in Barrington, Rhode Island. Though only sixteen years old, he served during World War II as a member of the United States Merchant Marine.

Barrington Connection

Earl was part of the Adams family of Bay Spring Avenue, a family that would suffer one of Barrington's most painful wartime losses. His older brother, Baker 3rd Class Albert W. Adams, also gave his life during World War II while serving aboard the submarine USS Trout.

Military Service

Earl R. Adams, Jr. served in the United States Merchant Marine, helping support the Allied war effort by transporting troops, supplies, fuel, and equipment across dangerous wartime seas.

Circumstances of Death

Earl served aboard the Liberty Ship SS Henry Bacon. The ship was attacked and sunk in the Barents Sea off the coast of Norway during World War II.

Earl R. Adams, Jr. lost his life aboard the SS Henry Bacon at only sixteen years old, making him one of the youngest Barrington residents to die in service during the war.

Family Sacrifice

Few Barrington families endured a greater wartime loss than the Adams family. Earl and his older brother Albert both died while serving their country during World War II.

Earl perished aboard SS Henry Bacon in the waters off Norway, while Albert was later lost aboard USS Trout in the Pacific. Their story remains one of the most poignant examples of sacrifice preserved in Barrington's military history.

Burial and Memorial

Earl R. Adams, Jr. is remembered among Barrington's World War II fallen and among the Merchant Mariners whose service and sacrifice were essential to Allied victory.
