Remembering John DiCicco

John DiCicco resided at 63 Hamilton Avenue in Barrington, Rhode Island. He was among the young men of Barrington who answered their nation's call during World War II.

Military Service

PFC DiCicco served with the 330th Infantry Regiment, 83rd Infantry Division during the Allied campaign in Western Europe.

Circumstances of Death

On July 18, 1944, PFC John DiCicco was killed in action during the Battle of the Hedgerows following the Normandy invasion of France.

The fighting in the Norman hedgerow country was among the most difficult combat faced by American forces after the D-Day landings, as soldiers fought through dense terrain defended by entrenched German forces.

John DiCicco was 19 years old.

Burial and Memorial

PFC DiCicco is buried at Santa Maria del Campo Cemetery in Barrington, Rhode Island.

Legacy

John DiCicco is remembered among Barrington's World War II fallen and among the many young Americans who gave their lives during the liberation of Europe.
