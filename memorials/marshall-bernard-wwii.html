Remembering Bernard J. Marshall

Bernard J. Marshall resided at 78 Walnut Road in Barrington, Rhode Island. He served with one of the most celebrated American units of World War II.

Military Service

PFC Marshall served in the 5307th Composite Unit (Provisional), commonly known as Merrill's Marauders.

Operating deep behind enemy lines in the Burma Campaign, Merrill's Marauders conducted long-range penetration operations against Japanese forces under extremely difficult jungle conditions.

Battle of Nhpum Ga

On April 8, 1944, Marshall was wounded during the Battle of Nhpum Ga in Burma.

During the battle, Merrill's Marauders killed approximately 400 Japanese soldiers while suffering 57 killed in action and 302 wounded. The unit frequently fought while outnumbered and isolated from conventional support.

Circumstances of Death

Following his wounding, Marshall was evacuated to the United States for medical treatment.

He later succumbed to his wounds on June 13, 1944.

He was 29 years old.

Legacy of Merrill's Marauders

Merrill's Marauders earned a distinguished place in American military history for their operations in Burma. The unit's lineage is carried on today by the 75th Ranger Regiment.

Their wartime exploits were later depicted in the 1962 motion picture Merrill's Marauders.

Burial and Memorial

PFC Marshall is buried at Long Island National Cemetery in New York.
