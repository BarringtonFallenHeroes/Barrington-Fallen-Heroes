Remembering Victor P. Andreozzi

Victor P. Andreozzi was born on October 1, 1946, to a second-generation Italian American family from Barrington, Rhode Island. He grew up on Bowden Avenue and enjoyed swimming and fishing in the Barrington River.

Barrington Connection

Victor was the oldest of six children and was looked upon as a second father by his younger siblings. He loved his MGA Roadster and was remembered for jumping off the White Church Bridge, now associated with the Barrington Veterans Memorial area.

Military Service

Victor enlisted in the United States Marine Corps on his eighteenth birthday. During the summer of 1967, he deployed to Vietnam with 2nd Battalion, 4th Marines, 3rd Marine Division.

Personal Life

Victor had made plans to marry his girlfriend Sharon, who was also a Marine, when he returned from Vietnam.

Circumstances of Death

On September 21, 1967, LCpl Andreozzi was killed in action during a ferocious battle just east of Con Thien and only a few kilometers south of the Demilitarized Zone.

He was killed just nine days before his twenty-first birthday.

Burial and Memorial

LCpl Victor P. Andreozzi is interred at Santa Maria del Campo Cemetery in Barrington, Rhode Island.
