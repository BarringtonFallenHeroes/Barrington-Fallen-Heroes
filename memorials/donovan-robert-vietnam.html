Remembering Robert Donovan

Robert “Bob” Donovan was born on April 8, 1946, to a second-generation Irish American family from Barrington, Rhode Island. He grew up on Nayatt Road and graduated from Barrington High School in 1964.

Barrington Connection

At Barrington High School, Donovan was an outstanding runner who lettered in track and cross country. His classmates knew him as “Bob” and “the Viking,” and voted him “Wittiest.” The Barrington High School Athletic Department later honored his memory with an annual award given to the school’s top runner.

Military Service

Donovan attended the University of Rhode Island for several years before enlisting in the United States Army. In March 1967, he graduated from Armor Officer Candidate School at Fort Knox.

In January 1968, he was assigned to Vietnam as a tank unit commander with Headquarters and Headquarters Company, 2nd Battalion, 34th Armor, 25th Infantry Division.

Circumstances of Death

Robert Donovan died on May 15, 1968, from multiple fragmentation wounds sustained during hostile action in Binh Duong Province, South Vietnam. He was 22 years old.

Burial and Memorial

He is interred at Gate of Heaven Cemetery in East Providence, Rhode Island.
