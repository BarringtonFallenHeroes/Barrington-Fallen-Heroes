Remembering Iver Johnson

Iver Johnson was born on February 12, 1897, in Pontiac, Rhode Island, a village of Warwick. A first-generation Swedish American, he later moved with his family to Barrington, Rhode Island.

Barrington Connection

Johnson entered military service from his parents' residence at 18 County Road in Barrington.

Prior to enlisting, he was employed by the Warren Manufacturing Company, now known as the former American Tourister Mill complex.

Military Service

On May 28, 1918, Johnson enlisted in the United States Navy during the height of America's involvement in World War I.

He served as a Seaman 2nd Class and was stationed in Newport, Rhode Island.

The Influenza Pandemic

In 1918, a global influenza pandemic swept through military camps, naval stations, troop ships, and civilian communities around the world.

While serving in Newport, Johnson contracted influenza and was transferred to Chelsea Naval Hospital in Massachusetts for treatment.

Circumstances of Death

Seaman 2nd Class Johnson succumbed to influenza on September 16, 1918.

He was among the thousands of American service members who lost their lives not to enemy action, but to disease during World War I.

The influenza pandemic ultimately claimed the lives of more than 45,000 American service members and accounted for nearly forty percent of all U.S. military fatalities during the war.

Burial and Memorial

Johnson's body was returned to his family and he is buried in the South Burial Ground in Warren, Rhode Island.

Legacy

Iver Johnson's story serves as a reminder that the sacrifices of World War I extended beyond the battlefield. Disease claimed nearly as many lives as combat and touched virtually every military installation in the United States and overseas.

He is remembered among Barrington's World War I fallen and among the many young Americans whose service was cut short by the influenza pandemic of 1918.
