Remembering Delfino Broni

Delfino Broni was born on July 2, 1893, in Rome, Italy. As a young single man seeking a better life, he immigrated to the United States through Ellis Island in May 1910.

Barrington Connection

Broni was sponsored by his uncle, Luigi Gasparro, and took up residence on Maple Avenue in Barrington, Rhode Island.

According to his June 1918 draft registration card, he worked as a brick cutter for the Barrington Steam Brick Company.

Military Service and Citizenship

The specific unit in which Delfino Broni served has not yet been confirmed through available records.

While stationed at Fort Meade, Maryland, awaiting deployment overseas, Broni applied for expedited naturalization based on his military service, as many legal aliens serving in the armed forces did during World War I.

Military records indicate that he received United States citizenship on November 1, 1918, just ten days before the end of hostilities.

Memorial Record

Although the precise circumstances of his death have not yet been confirmed, Delfino Broni's name is enshrined on Barrington's World War I memorial plaque.

His inclusion on the memorial indicates that he was recognized by the community among those who gave their lives during the World War I era.

Legacy

Delfino Broni's story reflects the service of immigrants who answered America's call during World War I and, through their military service, sought full membership in the nation they had chosen as their home.

Having received his citizenship only days before the Armistice, Broni appears never to have fully enjoyed the fruits of his newly attained nationality.
