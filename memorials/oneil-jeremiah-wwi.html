Remembering Jeremiah O'Neil

Jeremiah O'Neil was born on February 22, 1888, in County Cork, Ireland. At the age of eight, he immigrated to the United States with his family, eventually settling in West Barrington, Rhode Island.

Barrington Connection

O'Neil resided on Lake Avenue in West Barrington and was employed by the O'Bannon Corporation Mill, once the nation's largest manufacturer of imitation leather and today the site of the Barrington Cove Apartments.

According to his draft registration records, he was among the many Barrington residents who answered the nation's call to military service during World War I.

Military Service

O'Neil enlisted in the United States Army and served with Company A, 7th Battalion, United States Guards.

The mission of the United States Guards was to provide stateside security for critical military installations, armories, depots, and coastal defense facilities during the war.

Units such as O'Neil's helped safeguard key installations including facilities like Fort Adams in Newport and the Watertown Arsenal in Massachusetts, allowing combat units to deploy overseas.

Circumstances of Death

On November 25, 1918, shortly after the signing of the Armistice, Pvt. O'Neil was killed in the line of duty.

Military records indicate he sustained fatal skull and spinal fractures while serving with the United States Guards.

Family

Jeremiah O'Neil left behind his son, Jeremiah O'Neil, Jr.

His sacrifice serves as a reminder that military service continued to carry risk even after the guns fell silent on the Western Front.

Burial and Memorial

Following his death, O'Neil's body was returned to his family and interred at Mount Saint Mary's Cemetery in Pawtucket, Rhode Island.

Legacy

Jeremiah O'Neil is remembered among Barrington's World War I fallen and among the soldiers whose service protected vital military infrastructure during a time of national emergency.

Though he never served on the battlefields of Europe, his death in the line of duty reflects the many sacrifices made by service members supporting the war effort at home.
