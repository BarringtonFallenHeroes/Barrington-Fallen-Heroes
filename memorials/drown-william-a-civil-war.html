Remembering William A. Drown

William A. Drown served as a private in Company H, 3rd Rhode Island Cavalry during the Civil War.

Military Service

Drown enrolled for service on March 24, 1864, and was mustered into Company H, 3rd Rhode Island Cavalry on April 15, 1864.

Combat Service

Pvt. Drown was engaged in action at Napoleonville, Louisiana, on November 1, 1864.

He was also engaged at Doyall's Plantation, Louisiana, on November 29, 1864.

Circumstances of Death

On December 3, 1864, only days after the action at Doyall's Plantation, Pvt. William A. Drown died of disease at Donaldsonville, Louisiana.

He was 23 years old.

Burial and Memorial

Pvt. Drown is buried in Allin Yard Cemetery.

Legacy

William A. Drown is remembered among Barrington's Civil War fallen and among the Union soldiers whose service carried them far from Rhode Island into the campaigns of the Gulf and Mississippi River regions.
