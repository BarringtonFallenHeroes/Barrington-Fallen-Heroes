Remembering Joseph A. Chedell, Jr.

Joseph A. Chedell, Jr. was a native of Barrington, Rhode Island, who gave his life while serving the Union during the American Civil War. Remembered by his comrades as capable, courageous, and deeply respected, he was among the most highly regarded officers produced by the 1st Rhode Island Cavalry.

Early Life

At the outbreak of the Civil War, Chedell was studying medicine in the office of Dr. Newell of Providence. Inspired by duty to country, he enlisted alongside his mentor and entered military service with the 1st Rhode Island Cavalry.

Military Service

Chedell initially served as a Hospital Steward and was mustered into service on December 14, 1861.

His diligence, intelligence, and devotion to duty quickly earned the respect of both officers and enlisted men. Recognizing his leadership potential, the Army commissioned him as a Second Lieutenant on January 1, 1863, and he was formally mustered into that rank on January 14, 1863.

Campaigns and Battles

Lieutenant Chedell participated in numerous cavalry operations, scouts, and skirmishes throughout Virginia.

Among the major engagements in which he served were the Battle of Kelly's Ford, the Battle of Chancellorsville, the Battle of Brandy Station, and the Battle of Middleburg.

Brandy Station, fought on June 9, 1863, remains the largest cavalry battle ever fought on the North American continent and marked the emergence of Union cavalry as a formidable fighting force.

Circumstances of Death

On June 18, 1863, during operations near Middleburg, Virginia, Lieutenant Chedell was killed in action while serving at the head of his troops.

He fell only days after Brandy Station and just weeks before the pivotal Battle of Gettysburg.

His comrades remembered that he died exactly as he had lived— faithfully performing his duty and leading from the front.

Burial

Lieutenant Chedell was initially buried near the battlefield where he fell.

When Union forces later returned to the area, members of his regiment carefully exhumed and reburied his remains. They were subsequently transferred to Washington by the government before finally being returned to Rhode Island by family members.

Funeral services were conducted in Providence on December 9, 1866. Veterans of the 1st Rhode Island Cavalry, including many of his former comrades, attended the ceremony. Escorted by the Providence Horse Guards, his remains were laid to rest in the North Burial Ground in Providence.

A Soldier's Reputation

The official history of the 1st Rhode Island Cavalry described Lieutenant Chedell as a young officer of exceptional character, noting that he was "capable and beloved" and honored by all who knew him.

His fellow soldiers remembered him as a patriot, a soldier, and a Christian whose life was marked by devotion to duty, personal integrity, and selfless service.

"Few young men have a purer, brighter record than Lieutenant Chedell."

Legacy

Joseph A. Chedell, Jr. is remembered among Barrington's Civil War fallen and among the officers of the 1st Rhode Island Cavalry who helped preserve the Union during the nation's greatest crisis.
