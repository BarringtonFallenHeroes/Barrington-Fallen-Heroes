Remembering John Edward Brendle

John Edward Brendle was the son of German immigrants and served as an Ordinary Seaman in the United States Merchant Marine during World War II.

Barrington Connection

Brendle left behind his wife, Mrs. Bertha Farrol Brendle, who was residing with her uncle, Homer Giquere, at 127 Narragansett Avenue in Barrington, Rhode Island.

Military Service

Brendle served aboard the steamer SS Gurney E. Newlin, which was sailing in convoy to Manchester, England.

Circumstances of Death

On October 29, 1942, SS Gurney E. Newlin was torpedoed by German submarine U-436 in the North Atlantic.

Three men were killed in the initial attack. Brendle survived the first sinking, but the rescue ship, SS Bic Island, was torpedoed the following night. Forty-five men lost their lives at sea.

John Edward Brendle was 41 years old.

Burial and Memorial

John Edward Brendle is remembered among Barrington's World War II fallen and among the Merchant Mariners who lost their lives carrying men and material across the Atlantic.
